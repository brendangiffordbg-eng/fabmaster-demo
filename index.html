FM-INSP · How it works

Six steps. Zero surprises.

Every piece of equipment on a customer's site gets a tag, a schedule, and a paper trail — and the cycle repeats every month the customer stays enrolled.

STEP 01

Tag equipment

Every leg, conveyor, ladder and guard gets a numbered Fabmaster tag and a documented baseline condition.

STEP 02

Inspect assets

Scheduled PMIs cover safety, structure, wear parts and vibration — nobody waits for a breakdown.

STEP 03

Upload notes & photos

Findings go into the system on site — photos, measurements, and the millwright's notes, per asset.

STEP 04

Send the customer report

A plain-language report lands in the portal: what's fine, what's wearing, and what can't wait.

STEP 05

Schedule repair work

Findings become quotes, and quotes become booked off-season work — not mid-harvest emergencies.

STEP 06

Keep customers enrolled

The monthly program keeps assets on cycle — and keeps Fabmaster on site all year, not just in emergencies.

FM-SAFE · What we inspect

Safety compliance, in writing.

Our staff are trained in working at heights, confined space, lockout/tagout, WHMIS, aerial lift and fall arrest — and every inspection covers the items your insurer and the Ministry care about.

  • Machine guardingBelts, drives, pinch points — guarded, secured, documented.
  • Structural conditionLegs, towers, supports and catwalks checked for corrosion, cracking and fatigue.
  • Ladders & platformsAccess equipment verified against current safety standards.
  • Fall arrest pointsAnchors and tie-offs inspected and logged per asset.
  • Vibration analysisBearings and drives screened so failures show up in a report, not at 2 a.m.
